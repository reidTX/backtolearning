Due to the overlapping sport seasons through the current mainstream sports this allowed for year round entertainment, which the television in the home also assisted with. Individuals enjoyed having something to look forward to.

Wilma Rudolph

The 1960s olympic games proved Rudolph to be a unique individual as she overcame childhood Polio, and rose to become a world-record-holding Olympic champion and international sports icon in track and field.

The First Superbowl

The first AFL–NFL World Championship Game was an American football game played on January 15, 1967, at the Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum in Los Angeles, California. The National Football League (NFL) champion Green Bay Packers defeated the American Football League (AFL) champion Kansas City Chiefs by the score of 35–10.
