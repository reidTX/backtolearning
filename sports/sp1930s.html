The great depression and sports

Sports were a staple in during the recession as it gave individuals hope in unfrozen times. However, due to individuals being scarce on money attendance at the games dropped. Sports survived the depression, but similar to every business during the depression they suffered.

FIFA world cup

The 1930s FIFA world cup was an important one because it was the inaugural FIFA World Cup, the world championship for men's national football (soccer) teams. It took place in Uruguay from 13 to 30 July 1930. The 1930 FIFA World Cup final is the first and only one to date to have been contested between two Hispanic sides.

The NFL gains credibility

The Dayton team was the last original NFL franchise. They were eventually renamed the dodgers. The proceeds from the games in 1930 went to the New York Unemployment Fund to help those suffering because of the Great Depression, and the easy victory helped give the NFL credibility with the press and the public.
