New Human relatives

In 2010, scientists discovered the first remains of Denisovans in a Siberian cave. DNA extracted from a finger bone and a tooth revealed that Denisovans were genetically distinct from Neanderthals and humans, and diverged from the human family tree around 500,000 years ago.

First black hole image

The image was a major milestone in the study of black holes, which are thought to reside at the center of most galaxies. The image revealed the mass of the black hole at 6.5 billion times that of the Sun, and that it rotates in a clockwise direction.

CRISPR

A technology that research scientists use to selectively modify the DNA of living organisms. CRISPR was adapted for use in the laboratory from naturally occurring genome editing systems found in bacteria.
