Rise of streaming services

When Netflix made it big in the late 2010s, many families and households switched to streaming and canceled their monthly cable subscriptions. With cable and satellite prices soaring to $50 and $100 per month, many consumers were attracted to Netflix's $8.99 monthly subscription fee.

Mainstreaming queer cinema

In the early 2010s, some shows and films began featuring LGBTQ+ characters and storylines more prominently, breaking away from stereotypes and giving nuanced representation to queer experiences. Shows like Orange Is the New Black and Transparent not only brought LGBTQ+ lives into mainstream media but also invited audiences to connect with diverse, authentic stories.
