Silent film

A silent film is a film without synchronized recorded sound. Though silent films convey narrative and emotion visually, various plot elements or key lines of dialogue may, when necessary, be conveyed by the use of inter-title cards.

Film Studios

It was during the 1920's that studios became factories for large-scale productions of mass commercial entertainment. There were three film giants at the beginning of the 1920's and they were Zukor's Famous Players-Lasky Corporation, Loew's, Inc., and First National. United Artists was formed in 1919 by four prominent film artists, D.W. Griffith, Charlie Chaplin, Mary Pickford, and Douglas Fairbanks. In 1924, Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer (MGM) emerged as a result of the merger of Metro Pictures, Goldwyn Pictures, and Louis B. Mayer Productions (Loew's Inc.)

Walt Disney

Like many others in the 1920s, Disney moved to California with dreams of success. Along with his brother, Roy, he started a small animation studio and cajoled his friend Iwerks to join them. On November 28, 1928, they premiered "Steamboat Willie," the cartoon that introduced the universally appealing Mickey Mouse to movie audiences and was also the first to fully incorporate synchronized sound.
