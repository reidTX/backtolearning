Rise of independent cinema

Independent cinema, also known as "indie filmmaking", began to rise in the 1990s. Independent films became successful at the box office and through film festivals. Some established actors, like John Travolta, Bruce Willis, and Tim Robbins, also found success in independent films.

Home video market

By the mid-90s, the home video market was so huge that revenue of home video sales was higher than income generated in movie theaters. And this trend only continued into the start of the 21st century, as a new gizmo took swing. The DVD — digital video disc — was created in the late 90s by the Japanese brand Pioneer.

Animation

One of the most significant technological advancements during this era was the development of computer graphics software. This software allowed animators to create highly detailed and complex characters and environments that were impossible to achieve with traditional animation techniques.
