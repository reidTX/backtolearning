First Transatlantic phone call

On January 7th of 1927 the first official transatlantic telephone call was made by W.S. Gifford and proved the invention worked when he called Sir Evely P. Murray on the new commercial circuit.

Adoption of the Radio

The Radio was adopted by the public in the United States and Europe in the 1920s, and its popularity grew rapidly and it began the golden age of radio.

Electronic automatic traffic signals

Detroit traffic officer William Potts created this three-color, four-way traffic signal in 1920. Previous illuminated signals used only red and green lights. This advancement allowed for easier flow of traffic all around the world.
