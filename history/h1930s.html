In 1930 the dust bowl began

Summed Up: The dust bowl was caused by changes in regional weather, federal land policies, and farm economics. It began in the 1930s and lasted for around a decade. The dust bowl claimed many lives due to agricultural industries, famine, and diseases the abundant amount of dust caused in the infected states. A few states that suffered were Texas, New Mexico, and Calardo. The tragedy lasted until 1936 and the refuges of the Dust bowl were known as “Okies”
